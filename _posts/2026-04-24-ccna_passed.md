Just got mine CCNA, what a journey ! Not gonna lie, staying consistent and learning everything was tough. I started 4 months ago, during which:
* lots of labbing (mostly JITL MegaLab)
* watching and reading material (**JITL** course + [**CBT Nuggets**](www.cbtnuggets.com) for WLC)
* taking notes (around 300 A4 pages) and rereading them, trying to explain concepts in my own words
* using Anki cards everyday (I suggest adding your own cards with problematic topics)
* doing practice tests ([**Boson**](https://boson.com) and **JITL**)

Huge thanks to [**Jeremy McDowell**](https://www.jeremysitlab.com/) for the course. It was excellent, and his Mega Lab is great preparation for the CCNA lab section. And even though there were moments when I thought “please, no more Anki cards” 😊  I still kept on going and I’ll keep using Anki going forward. My worst day had around 300 cards to review. If a brain can feel pain, that was definitely the day it did 🧠 

In the end, I feel much more confident with networking, routing, and switching. Compared to CompTIA Network+, this was a lot more practical and much deeper.

The amount of material can make you doubt yourself but just keep on grinding ⛏️ in your own tempo.
